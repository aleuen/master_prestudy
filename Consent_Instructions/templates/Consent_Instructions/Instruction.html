Welcome to our experimental study of decision-making. The experiment will last about 5 to 10 minutes. All the money you earn is yours to keep. You will be compensated $ {{ Constants.compensation }} for the successful completion of this experiment, and will additionally receive a game payment which will depend on your choices in the experiment.

The experiment consists of 4 successive rounds of investment decisions. You will have {{ Constants.endowment_tot }} points total to invest with as you see fit. {{ Constants.endowment_tot }} points are equivalent of $ 1. In each round you must decide whether you would like to invest up to {{ Constants.endowment }} points ($ 0.25) of this amount in the following lottery:

With a chance of 1/6 (16%) the lottery will “succeed” and you will make 7 times the amount you invested. With a chance of 5/6 (84%) the lottery will “fail” and you will lose the money you invested. The procedure in each round is the same.

First, you are assigned one success number between 1 and 6. It is displayed on the computer screen. Second, you enter the amount of points you would like to invest in the lottery. The amount can be up to {{ Constants.endowment }} points in each round. You will indicate the amount you would like to invest by moving a sliding pointer. You can invest an amount up to $0.25 in each round. The $0.25 per round is separate from the $0.30 completion payment.

Whether the lottery succeeds or fails will be determined as follows. First, in each round you will be assigned one “success number” between 1 and 6. It is displayed on the computer screen. Second, you enter the amount of points you would like to invest in the lottery. The amount can be up to {{ Constants.endowment }} points in each round. After you indicate the amount you would like to invest in the lottery, you will be taken to a page where a six-sided die is rolled. If the outcome is your “success number” (1/6 chance) then the lottery will succeed and you will make seven times the amount you invested. If the outcome is any other number (5/6 chance) then you will lose the amount you invested. Afterwards, you get a new success number and make the same decision in the next round.

At the end of the four rounds, your game payment will be the {{ Constants.endowment_tot }} ($ 1) you started with, plus your net earnings from investments. Note that net earnings from investments could be positive or negative. Your game payment will be delivered as a bonus.
